Black Panther

A "black panther" is not its own species—it's an umbrella term that refers to any big cat with a black coat. The coloring comes from a surplus of melanin, the pigment responsible for suntans, and an animal with the condition is known as "melanistic."

Also, just because black panthers are dark in color doesn't mean they don't have spots—they're just harder to see.
